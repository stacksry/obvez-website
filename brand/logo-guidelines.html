Identity system · v1.0

The Obvez Labs mark.

A quiet, exact identity built on the same Apple-pure visual system as the website — one geometry, one family, one neutral palette, one accent. Precision first. Ornament last.

01 · Primary mark

A squircle. Two letters. Nothing else.

The primary mark is an iOS-style squircle with the letters OL set in Inter 800 with tight tracking. Built in a 180 × 180 box with a 40‑unit corner radius (≈22%), so it reads correctly at every size — from a 16 × 16 favicon to a 1024 × 1024 app icon.

Primary · near-black squircle on white
Inverse · white squircle on near-black

Clear space

Reserve padding equal to the cap-height of the “O” (roughly one-quarter of the mark's width) on every side. Keep type, imagery, and graphic edges outside this frame.

x x x x

Minimum size

Don't use the mark smaller than 16 px digitally or 8 mm in print. Below 24 px, favor the simplified favicon with a 14‑unit corner radius.

02 · Wordmark

Set in Inter 700. Optically tightened.

The wordmark is pure typography — Inter 700 with −0.035 em tracking. No ornament, no rules, no italics. Use the wordmark in body contexts where an icon would be redundant.

05 · Typography

One family. Five roles.

Inter is the primary typeface with -apple-system, "SF Pro Display" fallback. Use weight and tracking — not color — to build hierarchy.

06 · Do & don't

Keep the geometry clean.

Consistency is built by subtraction. When in doubt, remove.

Do · Use on white, off-white, or full near-black only.
Don't · Place on busy gradients or saturated brand photography.
Do · Pair mark + wordmark at equal cap-height.
Don't · Change the typeface, slant, or weight.
Do · Keep the 40-unit (≈22%) corner radius for the squircle.
Don't · Swap the squircle for a circle, pill, or hard square.
Do · Flip to the inverse mark on dark backgrounds.
Don't · Add strokes, glows, drop-shadows, or bevels.
07 · Voice

Understated. Exact. Human.

Write the way the brand looks. Short declarative sentences. No superlatives. Lead with the science, then the service, then the certification. Use sentence case everywhere except the formal lockup.

Good

“NABL-accredited analytical testing. Stability chambers to ICH Q1A. Validated methods, on time.”

Avoid

“Obvez Labs is a world-class, cutting-edge, innovative CRO delivering unparalleled quality & unmatched service excellence!”
